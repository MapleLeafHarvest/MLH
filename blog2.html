Maple Leaf Harvest Joins Centre for Social Innovation

Date
Author

Maple Leaf Harvest is happy to become a member of the Centre for Social Innovation. As the focus of this Toronto-based group is to provide support for those trying to further public good, they were a perfect match for our organization. Not only do they provide much-needed physical space to Maple Leaf Harvest and many others who may not have their own, but they also rely on a culture of collaboration and build a community around their centres. Though they do not cater to any single type of group alone, the variety of charities, entrepreneurs, and non-profits that work with CSI help foster the innovation needed to tackle social issues in unique and powerful ways. Whether it's through workshops that help impart useful knowledge, or just mixers to get people talking about their ideas, the centre is dedicated to helping those who truly wish to help others. CSI relies on innovation on their own part as well; the space they provide extends from normal "office space" into more unique environments like kitchens that can really get people talking. After all, much of what Maple Leaf Harvest and many others are aiming to accomplish is often discussed over food just as much as over conference tables. With a goal to revitalize how food is distributed, why not start at the kitchen table? The Centre for Social Innovation has multiple centres located in Toronto, and one located in New York City.
