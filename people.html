People are in Need

From day-to-day hunger to long-term malnutrition, much of the population of Canada struggles to find healthy food. In fact, in 2013 HungerCount estimated that people seeking help at emergency food assistance agencies grew from 675,000 people in 2008 to 835,000 people in 2013. This astonishing increase reaffirms that Maple Leaf Harvest can help alleviate this pressing issue of people in need of food.

According to Food Banks Canada's HungerCount 2012 report 1 of every 8 Canadian families struggle to put food on the table. Almost half of the households assisted are families with children. In general, almost 40% of those helped by food banks are under the age of 18. Every month 93,000 people in Canada access a food bank for a first time.

In 2012 alone 55% of food banks have had to cut back the amount of food provided to each household. Nearly 60% continue to buy more food than usual to meet the need. 8% have been forced to close early or not open because of a lack of food. 14% ran out of food at some point in the past year.

Many more emergency feeding institutions are having trouble providing enough food for all the hungry in need in their area, especially the smaller ones which are the the major focus for Maple Leaf Harvest.
