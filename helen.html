Helen verDuin Palit

Founder and President
h.palit@mapleleafharvest.org

As Social Entrepreneur she pioneered the global perishable food recovery system that today will feed one million people throughout 1,317 cities of the world, 8 billion meals and counting since 1981. She managed Yale University’s Dwight Hall Soup Kitchen in New Haven, CT when in 1981 she organized the first food recovery program, New Haven Food Salvage Project. Designed the perishable food distribution system in which local food producers, restaurants and caterers could donate their unused food and feed hungry people in local shelters, soup kitchens and pantries. In 1982 at the request of Mayor Koch, she founded City Harvest in New York City. As the media reported the harvest success’s concerned people from other cities of America and the world wanted their own harvest. Helen trained and guided them to tailor their program to their community’s ethos and spirit. In 1989 President Bush Sr, named Helen as the “Fourth Point Of Light” in his Thousand Points of Light Community Service Award with the mandate to replicate the program in other cities that need it. In 1990 she established America Harvest - a nonprofit to teach and assist others with the proven Harvest methodology and trainings including for the founders of Moisson Montreal, Halifax and 6 other Canadian food banks in 1985. Helen assisted in the start ups of Philabundance in Philadelphia, 12 Baskets in Minneapolis, Angel Harvest in Los Angeles, Aloha Harvest in Honolulu, Kyoto Harvest in Japan, the Tafels in Germany and Oz Harvests in Australia. Moreover multiple other grassroot food recovery systems where inspired by her work all around the world, including in France, Fiji, Israel, and others. At the request of Canadians, Maple Leaf Harvest, non-for-profit corporation is based in Ontario to other Canadian cities to reduce the waste of good food while enabling neighbors to feed their neighbors.
